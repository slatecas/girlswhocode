Fossil Fuels

Almost 85% of the U.S. energy supply comes from coal, oil, and natural gases. These are all fossil fuels. These take more than a thousand years to form and they are not renewable. The population can do many things to conserve energy. You can do things such as turning off the lights when a room is not in use and replacing your light bulbs with fluorescent light bulbs. Use reusable glass plates and cups. When shopping, you can buy post-consumer manufactured goods made from recyclable material. Instead of driving to work/school, ride a bike or walk. This helps to conserve our fossil fuels. You should also avoid using nylon, instead you should use natural fibers. Another way to conserve fossil fuels is to power your home with solar energy.
