What's the Issue?

Every year at least 10,000 species go extinct. This rate is between 1,000 to 10,000 times higher than the natural extinction rate. This crisis is called the 6th Extinction or the Holocene Extinction. Because of deforestation and wildlife trade, humans have taken thousands of animal lives. This extinction is also causing a major loss in biodiversity. Biodiversity is the variation of life on the planet. As we lose more species every year, Earth's biodiversity begins to falter.
